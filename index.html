Creative Coding Projects

Solar Center

Spring 2024 · Creative Algorithms

Description

SOLAR CENTER is a simple and personalizable meditation guide that aims to help balance hectic lifestyles with mindfulness through breathwork and exercising creativity.

Motivation

As life grew busier than before, balancing so many responsibilities led me to forget the importance in self care and taking breaks. I wanted to create a design/visual/process that could focus my attention at something almost hypnotizing to relax hyper minds.

Inspiration

Seeing an 80s design of a pixelated horizon amidst finals season, I felt there was quite a peaceful tone to the simplicity of the graphic. I thought the style could be an interesting take on a meditation guide, contrasting yet oddly coinciding with the calming aspects of meditation.

Design & Implementation

The app was built in Java using the Processing library. It features a dynamic horizon that expands and contracts with the user's breath, a timer for meditation sessions, and customizable color schemes. I focused on creating smooth animations and an intuitive interface to enhance the user experience.

Class Coding Projects

Timely App

Spring 2025 · Object Oriented Programming

Description

Timely is an interactive app for organizing events and tasks in a planner. It provides a simple calendar view for all-day events and time blocks, plus a unique progress chart that visualizes your task completion in real time.

Motivation

Time management is a skill I've been working on to balance personal and school life. After trying multiple planners, I couldn't find one with everything I needed — so I built my own.

Design & Implementation

The back-end follows the MVC pattern with Observer and Facade patterns. I implemented the To Do List and Progress Chart components, while my partner implemented the calendar. Together, we completed a full code cleanup to ensure all lines were relevant and optimized.

Elevator Simulator

Spring 2025 · Object Oriented Programming

Description

A simulator program mocking different elevator types that service a building. Each elevator bas unique behaviors and a scheduler to determine which elevator answers each call.

Motivation

Learn to develop a program using Test Driven Development and Object Oriented Principles.

Design & Implementation

We used interfaces for Elevators and Schedulers for uniformity. I implemented the Express and Freight elevator classes, the Complex Scheduler, and contributed to the Simulator class — all with comprehensive tests.

Birthday Paradox

Spring 2025 · Object Oriented Programming

Description

Runs the "Birthday Paradox" experiment, calculating the probability of at least two people in the same room sharing a birthday.

Motivation

Focus on writing clean code and efficient tests using Mockito.

Design & Implementation

The Simulation class runs groups of 5 to 100 people. I implemented the Simulation, Trial, and Experiment classes along with their tests.

Visual Art Projects

Untitled

March 2026 · Relief Reduction

Ideation & Inspiration

My grandmother had told me a story that when she was young, about 4 or 5 years old, she walked home in the Philippines with her carabou passing a cave. In the cave she saw golden eyes of a tiger staring at her, and she ran home in fear. I wanted to imagine the moment she saw those eyes in curiosity.

Learning Outcomes

With this being my first time doing a relief reduction, I learned a great deal how to listen to the ink and the printing press. Along the way I made many mistakes, but learned how to muse the materials to my advantage. I also learned how to be more patient and meticulous, as the process of relief reduction is very unforgiving and requires a lot of attention to detail.

Did You Eat Yet?

April 2025 · Altered Book

Goal

Alter a book using its contents to reimagine a new meaning.

Ideation & Inspiration

The heart of many cultures is food. I decided to lean on my personal perspective of being an Asian American and the cultural significance of food in my family. I wanted to explore the idea of food as a form of love and care, and how it connects to my identity and relationships. The question "Did you eat yet?" is often a masked expression of care and concern in many Asian cultures though rarely is it directly said. I wanted to bring that question to the forefront and explore the deeper meanings behind it through this book, "New American Cooking Methods and Definitions".

Learning Outcomes

Bookmaking is something very precise and personal. The intention of the colors, type fonts, and size of the book all contribute to its larger truth and deeper meaning. Taking this class and learning these methods helped me further appreciate the smallest details and make decisions carefully.
